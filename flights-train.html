Smart Booking Strategies: Flights and the Haramain Train

When planning a trip from the UK, transportation will undoubtedly take up the majority of your budget. It takes adaptability and knowledge of local booking windows to beat the system.

Mastering UK-to-Saudi Flights

  • Ditch the Direct Route: Although Saudia and British Airways provide easy direct flights from London to Jeddah or Madinah, they are more expensive. Consider indirect routes with layovers via Gulf carriers (such as Oman Air, Gulf Air, or Qatar Airways) or European budget combos to save hundreds of pounds per ticket.
  • The Low Cost Carrier Route: Think about scheduling a ticket to a European hub or a location like Amman or Cairo, after which you can travel into Jeddah with regional low-cost carriers like Flynas, Flyadeal, or Wizz Air.
  • Fly into Jeddah, Out of Madinah (or Vice Versa): When you purchase an open-jaw ticket, it usually costs the same as a regular return ticket, but you avoid having to spend time and money traveling back across the nation at the end of your vacation.

Scoring Cheap Haramain High-Speed Train Tickets

During the sweltering summer months, the Haramain Train is the quickest and most comfortable method to go between Madinah, Jeddah, and Makkah.

  • Book Exactly 25 to 30 Days Early: There is a rolling release timetable for train tickets. Only the more expensive Business Class seats remain after the economy tickets swiftly sell out. When your travel date appears on the official website or app, set a calendar reminder to purchase your tickets.
  • Travel During Off-Peak Hours: The stations are much less congested, and trains departing early in the afternoon or very late at night are sometimes less expensive than those departing in the morning or evening.
